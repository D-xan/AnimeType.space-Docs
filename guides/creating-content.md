# ✍️ Creating Content: Posts, Theories, & Selections

## The Heart of the Community

AnimeType thrives on user-generated content. Whether you are sharing a quick meme, reviewing the latest episode of a seasonal anime, or writing a 1,000-word deep dive into manga lore, the content creation tools are designed to give you maximum flexibility. 

When you click the "Create" button, you will notice that AnimeType offers different categorization options and content types. Understanding how to use these effectively will ensure your content reaches the right audience.

## 📂 The Three Core Selections: Anime, Manga, & Post

To keep the platform organized and help users avoid spoilers, AnimeType requires you to select a category for your standard content. 

### 1. The "Anime" Selection
You should select the "Anime" category when your content strictly pertains to animated adaptations. 
- **Use cases**: Reviewing a specific episode, discussing animation quality, sharing clips, or talking about anime original soundtracks (OSTs).
- **Why it matters**: Users who only watch the anime (and haven't read the manga) frequently browse this category. By keeping manga spoilers out of the "Anime" selection, you help maintain a safe, spoiler-free environment for anime-only watchers.

### 2. The "Manga" Selection
Select the "Manga" category (which also encompasses Light Novels and Manhwa) when discussing written source material.
- **Use cases**: Discussing the latest raw chapters, sharing colored manga panels, analyzing light novel pacing, or talking about events that have not yet been animated.
- **Why it matters**: This signals to the community that the post contains advanced plot details. Users know that clicking into a "Manga" post means they are stepping into spoiler territory.

### 3. The "Post" (General) Selection
If your content doesn't fit strictly into Anime or Manga, select the general "Post" option.
- **Use cases**: Sharing cosplay photos, posting general anime memes, talking about anime merchandise, asking for recommendations, or making community announcements.

## 🧠 Crafting Fan Theories

Fan Theories are the crown jewel of AnimeType. We have built an entirely separate post type just for them. When you switch the composer from "Standard Post" to "Theory," you unlock specialized formatting and the unique Tri-State Voting system.

### How to Write a Great Theory
1. **Strong Title**: Your theory needs a hook. Instead of "My thoughts on One Piece," use "Theory: Why [Character] will betray the crew in the next arc."
2. **Clear Evidence**: A good theory isn't just a random guess; it's backed by evidence. Reference specific chapter numbers, link to screenshots of manga panels, or quote dialogue from the anime to support your hypothesis.
3. **Formatting is Key**: Use paragraph breaks heavily. A giant wall of text is difficult to read on mobile devices. Use bullet points to list your evidence clearly.

### The Theory Voting System
When you publish a theory, users won't just "Like" it. They will vote on it using our specialized system:
- **Agree**: They believe your evidence is solid and your conclusion is correct.
- **Disagree**: They believe there are flaws in your logic or contradictory canon information.
- **Interesting**: They might not believe it will actually happen, but they love the creativity of your idea.

*(For a deeper explanation of how the agreement percentage is calculated from these votes, please read our [Theories & Voting Guide](./theories-and-voting.md)).*

## 🏷️ Best Practices for Hashtags

Regardless of what type of content you create, hashtags are essential for discovery. 
- Always include the name of the series (e.g., `#Bleach`, `#AttackOnTitan`).
- Include the type of content (e.g., `#FanArt`, `#Review`, `#Cosplay`).
- Limit your tags to 3-5 highly relevant words. Spamming 20 unrelated tags will not help the algorithm push your content; in fact, it may flag your post as spam.\n
