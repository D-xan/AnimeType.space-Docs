# 🔍 Theories & The Voting System

## Elevating Anime Discussions

If you've spent any time in the anime community, you know that theorizing is half the fun. Who is the true villain? What does that cryptic post-credit scene mean? What power-up will the protagonist unlock next?

On generic social media, posting a detailed theory often falls flat because the only way for people to interact is by hitting a generic "Like" button. That's why AnimeType engineered the **Theory System**—a specialized post format designed exclusively for hypotheses, predictions, and lore analysis.

## 🧠 What Makes a Theory Post Different?

When a user creates a post and selects the "Theory" format, the entire UI changes to accommodate deep discussion. 
Instead of a simple Like and Comment bar, Theory posts feature a massive, color-coded dashboard. The most prominent feature is the **Percentage Ring**—a circular progress bar that visually represents the community's consensus on the theory at a single glance.

To generate this percentage, users interact with the theory using our unique **Tri-State Voting System**.

## 🗳️ The Three Voting Options Explained

When you read a theory on AnimeType, you are presented with three distinct ways to cast your vote:

### 1. Agree (✅)
**When to use it**: Select "Agree" when you have read the theory, evaluated the evidence provided by the author, and firmly believe that their hypothesis is correct or highly probable based on the current canon.
**The Impact**: This vote increases the "Agree" count and actively pushes the Agreement Percentage Ring higher (closer to 100%).

### 2. Disagree (❌)
**When to use it**: Select "Disagree" if you believe the theory is factually incorrect, contradicts established lore, or simply lacks the evidence required to be believable. 
**The Impact**: This vote increases the "Disagree" count and actively pulls the Agreement Percentage Ring lower (closer to 0%). *Note: We strongly encourage users who vote Disagree to leave a comment explaining their reasoning to foster healthy debate!*

### 3. Interesting (💡)
**When to use it**: This is the wildcard vote! Select "Interesting" when a theory is incredibly creative, funny, or thought-provoking, but you don't necessarily believe it will actually happen in the story. 
**The Impact**: This vote is entirely neutral. It increases the overall engagement count of the post (rewarding the author with points and visibility), but **it does not affect the Agreement Percentage Ring**. 

## 🧮 Understanding the Agreement Percentage

You might wonder why a theory with 100 "Interesting" votes, 10 "Agree" votes, and 0 "Disagree" votes shows a 100% Agreement Ring. 

This is an intentional design choice! The Agreement Percentage is calculated *only* using the polarizing votes (Agree vs. Disagree). Because "Interesting" is a neutral vote representing entertainment value rather than factual endorsement, it is excluded from the math. This ensures that the percentage ring always displays the true factual consensus of the community, without being skewed by people who just thought the post was a fun read.

## 🗣️ Fostering Healthy Debate

Theories are meant to be debated! AnimeType's comment section on Theory posts is designed for deep threading. If you disagree with a theory, don't just downvote and leave—jump into the comments and post your counter-evidence. Many users have earned massive amounts of reputation points simply by posting brilliant, well-researched counter-arguments in the comment sections of viral theories. 

Remember: Keep the debates respectful, focus on the lore, and have fun unraveling the mysteries of your favorite shows together!\n
